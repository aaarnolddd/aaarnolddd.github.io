Tamarind

Named after the first thing I saw on my desk

Sep. 07, 2019

The skin on my right foot big toe is dry and cracked, making it hard to even bend my toe without feeling any pain. But that aside, for a guy who always writes about not letting anxiety about the future rule him, I spent all of today thinking even more about the future. It's not long till I finish my Bachelor's Degree and begin looking for a full time job, and this seems like a huge step in my life. No more three-month long vacations, no mid-semester breaks, no relaxing. However, I am looking forward to being able to support myself and my family once I start earning a full time income.

It seems that as time goes on, the monetary system works even less in favour of the humans who created it. House prices are skyrocketing, while the median wage rises slowly over the years; making it incredibly difficult for some people to have access to even the most basic housing. I am fearful that I will be trapped in this system just as I see many people trapped around me today, a slave to money. Somewhere in my mind, I wish I could live a simpler life, free of these worries, the way nature intended, but I realise that it is money that improved our standard of living exponentially. Where did we go wrong? Nonetheless, I will have to put up with this no matter what my opinion is, unless a better solution comes around.

I have recently heard about things such as the 'two-day rule' which encourages making a commitment to doing some sort of exercise every second day at the least. I have been neglecting my health for a while now, not leaving my house and choosing to play video-games. I heard about this concept from Matt D'Avella who also produced some other videos which I really liked, Simple Steps to Financial Freedom being my favourite, which has motivated me to start thinking about my money more wisely. I have decided to try and implement these ideas in my life, let's see how this turns out.

Obsession

My addiction...

Sep. 30, 2019

21 days since I last wrote. I am stuck at a cross-road in my life. Some things seem out of my understanding, while others seem clear as light. I have a real problem with addiction, no matter which vice I pick, I just have an extremely addictive personality. I am easily obsessed.

I realised that I have been living with constant anxiety and distractions. I have failed to live in the present for most of my life, and I don't understand how it happened. I often fail to realise the blessing that is the present moment. I am caught up in anxiety and mind-loops, unable to enjoy my life, and it really drives me crazy. I stick to random thoughts and let them take over my mind, even when it is apparent that it is harming me. I can't continue to live like this, I need to make a change in my life. Although I don't understand how I can do this, I am going to make an active decision to enjoy every moment for what it is in the present. I will eliminate my anxiety.

I have recently taken a liking for architecture. I feel as if it would have been a more suitable career path for me as opposed to Civil Engineering, and I am afraid that maybe I shouldn't have jumped straight into uni at the end of high-school, simply believing that it was the 'right' thing to do. These are some things I need to work on, but doing some research I found that it is possible for me to pursue my dream without having to go through the tedious process of university again. I do not want to be the old guy in class, and this is another anxiety that has been plagueing me lately, even though I realize it is such a silly thing to worry about. However, it is people such as Peter Zumthor and Tadao Ando who became self-taught Architects, who give me hope that maybe I didn't entirely waste my time at university, as it is what got me interested in Architecture in the first place.

Now let's live our lives in the present moment, and see what happens. If you are reading this, I love you.
